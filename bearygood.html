Beary Good is an app that allows students to keep track of their tasks while encouraging them to stay productive.

Time Frame: 4 weeks Team: 4 Designers Role: Product Designer

The Problem

Cornell students often lack motivation, face burnout, and struggle with procrastination, which are challenges that are often exacerbated by external circumstances. Given the turbulent nature of COVID-19, these challenges become even more relevant as students learn to navigate their lives in nuanced ways.

We wanted to understand how to tackle this thought process and intervene with design. How can we both encourage productivity in our peers and other college students while challenging the mindset that people have to constantly be working and stressed?

Literature Review

What kind of data out there supports the existence of this problem? I've included an excerpt from our literature review below.

Excerpt:
How students feel and think about their college experiences impact how they pursue academic, social, and personal goals, thus healthy and positive attitudes are critical for confidence, motivation, and success. Nonetheless, most students’ college experiences are ridden with negative attitudes and practices, including procrastination, burnout, and lack of motivation, that not only harm external measures of success like academic performance but also trap students in toxic physio-psychological cycles that perpetuate such negative attitudes towards school and themselves (Cheng & Icks, 2009; Haycock et al., 1998; Smith & Dillard, 2006; Swan, 2001).

User Research

Findings: Students want to be motivated, see their efforts produce tangible results, and reflect on a well-rounded lifestyle.

Method:
To understand our users we decided to use a diary study as our primary method of user research. Our diary study was conducted using 3 surveys: (1) An introductory survey that gathered preliminary feelings related to productivity, (2) Daily diary entry surveys that allowed participants to reflect on their day and their productivity levels (3) A concluding survey.

These insights suggest that students tend to feel discouraged because they can't recognize their levels of productivity. Validating their achievements may encourage frequent productivity instead of spurts of energy as seen in certain responses. Furthermore, students want a sense of fulfillment through a balance of personal and school-related activities.

Design Process

After gathering our user research, we started to brainstorm.

Key Pain Points:

  • Students want a way to reflect on their day and understand their habits.
  • Students feel that starting projects is difficult which results in procrastination and stress.
  • Students want a balanced life where they can not only focus on schoolwork, but also their family, friends, and mundane tasks like chores.


    • Understanding these pain-points, we designed some key features (shown below). Based on our user research, we know it’s extremely important to help the user recognize their productivity, reward them for completing tasks, and provide incentives to do so. Additionally, we created a mascot as a part of gamification. This bear and apple theme was inspired by the Animal Crossing craze!

    Completing Tasks:
    Once a user finishes a task, the apple will disappear from the screen and the number in the honey pot will increment and the bear will grow as it has “eaten” the apple.
    From our user research, we noticed participants still wouldn’t feel productive even if they had been accomplishing tasks all day.

    To combat this, we designed each task accomplishment to be shown physically so that users can see a noticeable change and recognize their productivity.

    Once a user completes 20 tasks, a gift will pop up. These gifts will be items that the user can use to customize the bear.

    To-Do List:
    If a user would like to create more tasks, they can navigate to the tasks page, which will lead to a list of all their tasks and the percentage of how much they’ve accomplished for that task. The user can then add a task by giving it a name and a description, which will pop up if the user clicks on the apple corresponding to this task on the main page.

    We feel like this type of "to-do" list is more interactive and encouraging.

    Customization:
    The closet is organized by item. Clicking on an item would give the user the name, the date they received it, and what goal they accomplished.

    This feature gives the user a timeline of accomplishments since they can connect each reward to a time when they reached a goal.

    From the closet, the user can reach the "dressing room" where they can change the bear’s clothes, background, and accessories.

    This adds another level of personalization to the app so that users are more inclined to come back and take care of the bear that they have put so much effort into.

    Outcome and Next Steps

    We are proud of how this project turned out! Especially since we were moved to an online class, I feel like we were able to accomplish so much given the circumstances. I think the features we designed were based on the data that we had and targeted the emotions that we understood from the affinity diagram.

    For the next steps, we would test out the design with the stakeholders that we researched using the diary study. I feel like there are certain aspects of the features that we could clean up or iterate on. I would especially like to see how effective our theme (the bear and the apples) was in creating an encouraging and relaxing environment.
