Primary Objective

Our goal for the design is to encourage our users to see productivity in a more positive light and to create more positive attitudes towards completing schoolwork and other arduous tasks.

Design

We wanted to incorporate gamification into the app to encourage users to keep coming back and to have a consistent productivity streak rather than being productive in bursts. To do this, we decided to brainstorm ways for the user to earn rewards but we knew the rewards couldn't be tangible because that wouldn't be very feasible. We were inspired by a lot of "plant growing" apps where a user could "grow a plant" through staying off of social media for a certain amount of time.
These apps and about an hour of brainstorm led to: Your friendly neighborhood bear! We wanted to create a design that was friendly, welcoming, and fun to look at after a long day of schoolwork and day to day tasks. We felt that the bear would give us the most amount of customization abilities in terms of rewards. We were also inspired by Animal Crossing and its success amongst our peers and even people older than us.

If I could describe it in one sentence: Gamified To-Do List.

Features

Set Up and Create a Task

The user can then add a task by giving it a name and a description, which will pop up if the user clicks on the apple corresponding to this task on the main page.

Completing Tasks and Gaining Rewards

Once a user finishes a task, the apple will disappear from the page and the number in the honey pot will increment and the bear will grow as it has “eaten” the apple. Once a user completes 20 tasks, a gift will pop up. These gifts will be items that the user can use to customize the bear: shirts, bottoms, backgrounds, hats, and more.

Closet

Once tasks are completed and a user earns a gift, they can view it along with all their customization options in the closet.

Customizing

From the closet, the user can reach the customization feature where they can change the bear’s clothes, background, and accessories.
